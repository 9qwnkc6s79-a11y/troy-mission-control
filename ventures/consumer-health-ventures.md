### Idea #1: Non-Invasive Cortisol Wearable (Rank 17 ‚Äî Score 5.85)

**Market Size / TAM:** The stress management/monitoring market is ~$15B globally. Wearable biosensor segment for hormonal monitoring is emerging ‚Äî estimated $500M-$1B addressable by 2028. Corporate wellness alone is a $60B market. However, the *specific* cortisol wearable segment doesn't exist yet so TAM is theoretical.

**Competition:** No consumer product exists. Academic prototypes from Stanford (sweat-based electrochemical cortisol sensors) and Caltech published 2024-2025. Companies like Levels and Nutrix are working on sweat analytics but none have shipped a cortisol-specific product. Apple and Samsung have reportedly explored cortisol for future devices.

**Patent Landscape:** Multiple university patents on electrochemical cortisol sensing. Stanford holds key patents on aptamer-based cortisol sensors. Caltech has flexible sweat sensor patents. Could be blocking but licensable.

**Manufacturing:** OEM electrochemical sensor modules are available from Shenzhen suppliers (e.g., Giner Labs, Zimmer and Peacock). Wearable housing + BLE module is standard contract manufacturing. COGS estimate: $35-60 per unit.

**Regulatory:** If marketed as "stress awareness/wellness" = likely wellness device (FDA exempt). If claiming cortisol level measurement = FDA may classify as diagnostic (510(k) or De Novo). Strategy: Launch as wellness, avoid specific cortisol number claims.

**Time to First Revenue:** 18-24 months (sensor calibration and validation is the bottleneck)
**Capital Required:** $500K-$1M

**Market Size / TAM:** Global transdermal drug delivery market ~$8B (2024), growing 7.5% CAGR. Dietary supplement market ~$200B globally. DTC vitamin/supplement patches = convergence of both. Specific addressable: $1-3B.

**Patent Landscape:** Transdermal patch technology is mature and well-patented for pharma. Supplement patches use different IP (topical delivery, not transdermal in the pharmacological sense). Freedom to operate is good for supplement formulations.

**Manufacturing:** Contract manufacturers in China, South Korea, and India produce custom patch formulations. MOQ often as low as 10K-50K patches. Tooling cost: $5K-15K. Per-patch COGS: $0.50-1.50.

**Regulatory:** Dietary supplement patches fall under DSHEA (Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act). NOT regulated as drugs or medical devices if marketed as supplements. Very favorable regulatory environment.

**Time to First Revenue:** 3-6 months (fastest on the list)
**Capital Required:** $25K-75K for initial production run + branding
