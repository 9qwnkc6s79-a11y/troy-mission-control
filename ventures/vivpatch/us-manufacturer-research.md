## Key Outreach Template

When contacting manufacturers, ask about:

1. **Cosmetic classification experience** ‚Äî Have they made patches classified as cosmetics under FDA FD&C Act?
2. **MOQs** ‚Äî Minimum order quantities for first production run
3. **Custom formulation** ‚Äî Can they develop custom formulas, or do you need to bring your own?
4. **Who owns the formula** ‚Äî Critical for brand independence
5. **Certifications** ‚Äî GMP, FDA registration, ISO certifications
6. **Timeline** ‚Äî From first conversation to finished product
7. **Pricing structure** ‚Äî Per-patch cost at various volumes
8. **Packaging options** ‚Äî Individual pouches, sheets, boxes
9. **Printing** ‚Äî Custom branding on patches and packaging
10. **Fulfillment** ‚Äî Do they offer warehousing and direct-to-consumer shipping?

## Competitive Landscape Notes

- **PatchMD** retails at ~$25-40/30 patches ($0.83-1.33/patch retail)
- **Nutri-Patch** prices around $20-35/30 patches
- Both classify as **cosmetics** under FDA, not dietary supplements
- Key marketing language to note: "nourish your skin," "promote overall well-being" ‚Äî **NOT** "absorbs into bloodstream" or "treats/cures"
- The cosmetic classification means:
  - No FDA pre-market approval needed
  - Cannot make drug claims (treat, cure, prevent disease)
  - Must follow cosmetic labeling requirements
  - Lower regulatory burden than supplements or drugs
